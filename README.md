## Project Overview
End-to-end HR analytics project analyzing **1,470 IBM 
employee records** to identify key drivers of employee 
attrition. This Project demonstrates the complete data analytics workflow, from Python-based Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) and statistical hypothesis testing to an interactive Tableau dashboard, solving a real-world HR business problem.

---

## Problem Statement
Identify which factors (department, overtime, salary, 
job role) most significantly drive employee attrition, 
using statistical testing and visual analytics to 
support HR decision-making.

---

## Dataset
- **Records:** 1,470 employee records
- **Variables:** 35 columns
- **Source:** IBM HR Analytics Dataset (Kaggle)
- **Key Columns:** Age, Department, MonthlyIncome, 
  OverTime, JobSatisfaction, Attrition, JobRole, 
  WorkLifeBalance, YearsAtCompany

---

## Project Structure

### Part 1: Python EDA
**File:** `hr_attrition_analysis.ipynb`
- Data cleaning and quality validation
- 6 professional visualizations
- Key findings and business insights
- Tools: Python, Pandas, Matplotlib, Seaborn

### Part 2: Statistical Analysis
**File:** `hr_attrition_analysis.ipynb`
- SciPy t-tests on income, age, years at company
- Correlation heatmap (10 numerical variables)
- Confirmed income difference statistically 
  significant (p < 0.05)
- Tools: SciPy, NumPy, Seaborn

### Part 3: Tableau Dashboard
**File:** `hr_attrition_dashboard.twbx`
- 3 KPI cards (Total Employees, Left, Rate)
- 4 interactive bar charts
- 2 dynamic filters (Department, Attrition)
- Tools: Tableau Public

---

## Key Findings
1. Overall attrition rate: **16.12%**
2. Employees who left: **237 out of 1,470**
3. Highest attrition dept: **Sales (133 employees)**
4. Overtime attrition: **127 vs 110 (no overtime)**
5. Low salary = highest attrition: **163 employees**
6. Income difference: **Statistically significant 
   (p < 0.05)**
7. Younger employees leave more than retained ones
